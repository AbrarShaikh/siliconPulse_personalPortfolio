I write firmware that runs where reliability isn't optional — from silicon bring-up at ARM to production ECUs at BOSCH. I care about cycles, bytes, and the last microampere.

I’ve spent the last 4.7+ years working close to the metal — writing Embedded C for 16-bit automotive MCUs at BOSCH (NXP S12Z, AutoSAR MCAL/CDD, UDS/XCP), and now shipping HAL & bring-up infrastructure for Arm Cortex-A cores during silicon verification. Along the way I’ve fallen deep into optimization — from shaving 8 KB off a boot image to cutting 78% of RTL-sim runtime.

Outside the day job I explore hardware-software co-design — I’ve synthesized a RISC-V keypad driver down to SkyWater 130nm cells, and built a 2D convolution engine on FPGA for image processing. Silicon and software feel like the same problem to me.
